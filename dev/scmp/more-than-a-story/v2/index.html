With misinformation everywhere and our trust in institutions being eroded, now, more than ever before, the South China Morning Post needs to show our dedication to communicating fact-based and trusted reporting to our Hong Kong readers.

Independent fact-based journalism requires resources, time and commitment, which is why we go beyond simply reporting the news. Our 800+ team is made up of local and international people who genuinely care about our (and their) home city, and report on it with insight and nuance.

Our editorial narrative is more than just words on a page. SCMP’s reporting builds on our 118+ years of experience to deliver quality journalism that digs deep and goes further.

We strive to question answers and seek the truth, constantly aware of the role we play in rebuilding trust in the media, and help our readers better understand the pressing issues facing our city and the world.

Episode 1

Fiona Sun, Emily Tsang, and Zuraidah Ibrahim, introduce us to the city’s housing crisis. With 220,000 Hongkongers living in tiny and cramped subdivided flats, what are the key issues they face and how has it got to this point? Part one takes a closer look and talks to the people impacted.

Unpacking all parts of a story and rigorous fact-checking requires the highest levels of editorial integrity. That’s why in March 2020, the Post was proud to be the first Asian news organisation to join the Trust Project, a global network of leading media organisations committed to high levels of transparency and credible reporting.

“The Trust Project provides us with transparent standards of news production to help readers around the world who come to us for quality, trustworthy journalism” Tammy Tam, Editor in Chief.
